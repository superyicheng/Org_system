# org.system — 90-second introduction

This is spoken narration, meant to be read straight through in full sentences. For the
click-by-click recording script, use [DEMO_SCRIPT.md](DEMO_SCRIPT.md).

The thesis is that a company's accumulated experience is the most valuable asset it owns,
and that today its AI cannot reach it. Making that asset reachable raises the ceiling on
how large and how efficient an organization can become.

A few things to keep honest while delivering it. Sarah and Tom are demo identities, and
their numbers are seeded fixtures, so keep the interface on screen while you say them and
never present them as a measured customer result. Do not claim the system stores
everything or learns every detail, because it stores a redacted, distilled experience,
which is what AGENTS.md Rule 4 and the README commit to. The closing point about growth is
the conviction behind the product rather than a benchmark, so deliver it as a belief.

---

*[Open on the Live proof view, with the counter reading zero lab days.]*

> Last night, Sarah's iGEM team finished a GFP promoter screen. They ran a full
> ninety-six-well plate, and it took them six days at the bench. The assay failed, because
> the fluorescence was so noisy, at thirty-eight percent variation, that they could not
> tell the four promoters apart. They had spent six lab days and had nothing they could
> compare. This morning, Tom sat down to plan the same whole-plate assay, and his AI told
> him to go ahead, because it had no idea that yesterday had happened.

*[Cut to a lab notebook that was last edited weeks ago, then pull back to a room of people at benches.]*

> Companies already have records that can feed an AI, such as wikis, documents, and
> tickets. But people wrote those for other people, and they hold only what someone chose
> to write down. To capture a lesson, a person has to decide the work mattered, remember
> it accurately days later, and boil it down into a few lines. Almost nobody does that for
> the experiments that failed, so the most expensive lessons a team owns are the ones that
> are least likely to survive.

*[Tom's proposal types itself in, and the receipt naming Sarah appears beside it.]*

> This is a new kind of asset. org.system captures the work as it finishes, taking it from
> the session itself and redacting it while the detail is still there, so it keeps the
> measurements, the attempts that were abandoned, and what the work actually cost. It
> shapes that into something an AI can use directly, rather than a page that has to be
> found, and your AI checks it before it commits to anything. So when Tom begins, his AI
> finds Sarah's result, with her name still on it, before he spends a thing.

*[Switch to the Team map and let the link from Sarah to Tom draw itself.]*

> This is what changes as a team grows, because people cannot know what everyone else has
> already learned. When every AI carries that memory, Sarah's failure becomes Tom's
> correct decision, and the team stops paying for the same lesson twice.
